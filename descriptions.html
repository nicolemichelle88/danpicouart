Witch

This sorceress can control magics that take mortals a lifetime of study and dedication to master. Her age is unknown, but her penchant for necromancy suggests she has used her power to greatly extend her life.

She can reanimate the dead to do her bidding and even manipulate their flesh to create unique, more powerful creatures. Forest vegetation can be manipulated as well; normally this elemental magic can only minimally accelerate growth, but by controlling some aspects of time, she can animate the forest elements and use them as weapons.

Her body is invulnerable to fire due to her extensive study. As with most sorceresses who specialize in flame magic, she suffered from burns and scars in her younger years until she achieved mastery of flame; however, she subsequently has achieved advanced healing powers, allowing her to reverse the damage. She likely began her study into elemental magic to defend herself from witch hunters who burned their victims at the stake.

Homo celestian

A future branch of human evolution, these organisms have evolved to adapt to a weightless environment. Unlike what is observed in their ancestral species, Homo sapiens, a large lung capacity is no longer necessary, resulting in a marked decrease in the volume of the torso. Likewise, muscle mass and bone density have also decreased, as these structures are no longer advantageous. Much of the digestive tract has also become vestigial, and the mandible and maxilla are largely fused; their low energy requirements allow them to subsist on very little nutrition.

Throughout their years inhabiting space, Homo celestian has become increasingly dependent on their technology, essentially replacing many of their sensory organs. Visual information is now gathered and processed by the ship and fed to the nervous system, allowing these creatures to have a mental schematic of the ship at all times. For this reason, their eyes are also vestigial with greatly reduced orbits. Their understanding of space itself would be foreign to us; having lived their lives without a planet and the relative perspective such an environment would provide, they have no sense of “up” or “down.” As a result, it is suspected that their understanding of the fundamental nature of the universe greatly surpasses our own. However, this adaptive strategy comes at a price: they are completely dependent on their technology and cannot survive without the aid of their ships or suits.

Despite their aplanetary life-strategies, these organisms still visit planets periodically to mine the resources they use for the construction of their technology. The suits alone are marvels of engineering; each one is built with synthetic muscles that attach to an exoskeleton that precisely layers over and corresponds to their natural skeletal system’s muscular insertion landmarks. These suits give them exceptional strength and athletic ability; however, since they have become so dependent on the ship for their sensory information, expeditions off-ship require extensive training on the use of the suit’s sensory input mechanisms.
